Why Google is evil

Jake Rayson

Web designer builder

growdigital.org/why-google-is-evil

Some facts

  • Google’s corporate motto is “Don’t be evil”
  • Google’s business model is advertising.
  • They harvest your data.
  • The same goes for Facebook, Amazon, Twitter, Microsoft, Yahoo! etc.
  • Free is a lie

The problem: concentration of power

  • Early Stalin nicknamed “The List Maker”
  • Data harvesting essential to Google et al
  • A business model predicated on exploitation of data and consolidation of power
  • Centralised data, centralised power
  • One stop shop for government spying

What to do: 1. alternative functionality

  • Search: DuckDuckGo
  • Maps: OpenStreetMap
  • Chat: IRC using eg Colloquy
  • Email, Calendar, Contacts: FastMail ($40 per year). Or DIY!
  • Twitter: RSS news feed reader, like Vienna or Thunderbird. Webmentions.
  • Analytics: Piwik open source website statistics
  • Books: World of Books
  • BE AWARE

What to do: 2. protective software

  • Linux: Free operating system eg Ubuntu or Linux Mint
  • TLS (was SSL): Transport Layer Security, always visit https:// websites
  • Tor: anonymous online browsing
  • Ghostery: block untrusted tracking by websites
  • NoScript: prevent unwanted scripts in Firefox
  • Disk encryption: to block unwanted access to your hard disk
  • Firewall: to prevent unwated access via the network
  • BitTorrent Sync: to share files between computers & devices (rather than Dropbox)
  • SSH: Secure Shell for logging in to servers. Set up keys!
  • GPG: GNU Privacy Guard, for sending secure emails (Mac GPG tools)
  • KeePassX: cross-platform password manager
  • Adblock Plus: blocks ads

What to do: 3. alternative strategy

  • Own your data.
  • Own your domain name. Use trustworthy registrar eg gandi.net
  • Publish Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere
  • See Indie web building blocks
  • LEARN MORE

What to do: 4. alternative platforms

  • Decentralised
  • Simple
  • Ind.ie phone, Pulse file sync, Heartbeat social network
  • Sandstorm.io easy web app platform
  • Listen
